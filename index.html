Capstone Reflection

Hi, my name is Stan H, and I am a Computer Science student with a strong interest in IT, networking, and secure system design. Throughout my time in the Computer Science program, I have developed a solid foundation in programming, problem-solving, and security-focused thinking.

One of the biggest areas I improved in was communication. I learned how to explain technical concepts clearly, which is important when working with team members or stakeholders who may not have a technical background.

My understanding of algorithms and data structures improved through projects where I applied logic step by step. In my SQL injection detection program, I used a vector of patterns and looping logic to improve how attacks are detected.

I also developed stronger skills in software design and databases by organizing my code into smaller functions and implementing prepared statements to prevent SQL injection. This helped me understand how to build more secure systems.

Security became a major focus for me. I learned to think beyond just making programs work and instead focus on how to prevent vulnerabilities. This mindset is important in real-world IT and development roles.

The artifacts in this portfolio reflect my growth. My code review shows my ability to analyze code, while my enhancements demonstrate improvements in software design, algorithms, and database security. Together, they show how I can take an existing system and improve it step by step.

Overall, this program has prepared me to enter the IT field with a stronger understanding of systems, security, and problem-solving. I am confident in my ability to continue learning and apply these skills in a professional environment.
